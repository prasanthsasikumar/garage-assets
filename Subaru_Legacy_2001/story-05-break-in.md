# The Break-In Incident

The Subaru Legacy is one of the most stolen cars in New Zealand. It is unassuming, it has enough power to outrun trouble, and above all it is famously easy to steal. I knew all this when I bought one. You always know, right up until it happens to you.

Someone had been watching the car for a while. They had studied my routine at the lab: home for dinner in the evening, back for late nights through the winter months. One evening I came in at my usual time, felt unwell about ten minutes in, and decided to head home early. In the car park there were components lying on the ground beside the car, and the door was unlocked. Inside, the ignition barrel was smashed apart and a fistful of wires had been cut. The car had a highly rated alarm; somehow they had got past it.

What probably saved the Legacy is a safety switch tucked under the clutch that only triggers on a very hard press. They seem to have missed it, and spent their time rewiring things instead until they gave up, or until I nearly walked in on them. I called my friends at the lab and we towed the disabled car home.

Over the following days I turned the whole mess into a repair project. I went to Pick-A-Part for a replacement ignition barrel and learned how to extract one from a donor car, which involves chiselling away at a security screw designed specifically to stop people doing what I was doing. Then I rewired the damaged loom and got the car running again. The thieves got nothing, and I got another skill I never planned on having.
