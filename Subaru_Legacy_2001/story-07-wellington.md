# Life in Wellington

In 2018 I moved to Wellington for work, on a contract with Te Papa, the national museum of New Zealand. When the contract ended, the income ended with it. Job applications went nowhere, so I stacked up whatever work I could find, including a stint at PlaceMakers, and slowly paid off my debts. Before that gig came through, though, there was a stretch with no money at all. Even at the peak of it, genuinely hungry, I did not sell the car. I kept telling myself the rough patch would pass and the Legacy would still be there on the other side of it. It was. That era is also when the car finally got the BBS wheels I had always wanted; upgrades happened whenever I could afford them, which was rarely, which made each one sweeter.

## Stashing the Car Back in Christchurch

Then Wellington handed me the opportunity of a lifetime: an offer to do a PhD with Mark Billinghurst, the father of Virtual Reality. I took it without hesitation. I was still broke, and keeping a car in Auckland was out of the question, so a friend offered their place in Christchurch as parking while I sorted my life out. I drove the Legacy down, said my goodbyes, and flew north.

Life got busy the way a PhD makes sure it does, and the car stayed in the South Island for about a year. I made it back once, just long enough to start it and move it around. There was another break-in attempt during that time too, but all they took was the boost gauge. The car, patient as ever, waited.
