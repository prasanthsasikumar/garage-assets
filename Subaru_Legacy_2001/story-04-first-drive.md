# First Drive & Adventures

## First Drive

I still remember the first time I keyed the ignition. I had disabled the fuel pump to prime the engine, and the moment I reconnected it, the car fired straight up. The drive to the fuel station was spectacular. I had never driven anything with that much power and character, and I could not quite believe that everything I had done actually worked.

The next night I took it out properly. The car had no Warrant of Fitness or registration yet, so I waited for dark, when fewer police would be around. On an empty stretch I floored it and felt the turbos spool one after the other, and the speedo passed 180 before I had finished processing what was happening. I had never gone that fast in my life. I grinned the whole way home. Those moments laid the foundation for every project that came after.

The sensible bits followed: a new windscreen, a few minor fixes, and a passed warrant. Then the fun bits. The car came along on adventures to most corners of the South Island. I fitted boost gauges, tried different blow-off valves chasing that perfect "stuu tuuu" between gears, and replaced the exhaust. Later, once I finally had some money, I started buying other Legacies whenever they came up cheap, purely to swap the interesting bits across to this one. The car had stopped being a purchase and become a platform.
