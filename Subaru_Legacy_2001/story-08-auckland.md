# Moving to Auckland

Right after Covid I was finally able to bring the car up from Christchurch to Auckland. By then our place had about eleven cars parked at it at any given time, and the neighbours had started to wonder what our real jobs were. The Legacy went back to being the daily driver, and life was good: late-night drives, car meetups, and the small milestone of affording Pirelli tyres for the first time. Used ones, but Pirellis.

The trouble with daily driving a car you love on a PhD budget is that the car keeps needing things the budget cannot supply. Services started slipping. A sound began coming from the gearbox now and then, and I could feel a faint binding through the driveline in certain corners. I never did the inspection. Why fix something that is not broken, the saying goes. That saying was about to cost me, and set up one of my greatest stories yet.
