# Keeping the Car in Storage

As the PhD wound down, I decided to leave New Zealand and go see the world. That meant selling everything. And I mean everything: the engine cranes, the stands, the compressors, the spare engines, the multiple copies of most tools that years of projects had accumulated. One by one it all went. The only thing I chose to keep was the Legacy.

I found a storage spot out in Hamilton, about an hour from Auckland. I cleaned the car up properly, drove it to Mohsen's house with a heavy heart, and we loaded it onto his truck one last time and took it to where it sits now. Saying goodbye is always hard, and it is hardest when you don't know how long it will be until you meet again. There is a little underbody rust starting to show, and I hope it holds off. My hope is to earn enough to ship the car to whichever country I land in, restore it to its former glory, and make it my daily once more.

## What is in for the Future?

Future plans are always a mystery, but this one is fully specced out in my head. The dream is to move to the US someday, base my company there, and import the Legacy under the vintage vehicle rule; as a 2001 model, it qualifies in 2026. The build would be a full strip, sandblast and repaint, either the original colour or grey, plus a Rev C body kit and a built engine making around 450 horsepower, reliably. But the twin-turbo setup stays, valley of death and all: that strange dead moment while the small turbo hands over to the big one, the flaw that gives the car its character. Everyone else engineers their quirks out. This car keeps every single one.
