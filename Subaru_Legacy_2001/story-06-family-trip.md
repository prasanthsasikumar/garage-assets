# Trip with Parents

My family came to visit me in Christchurch, and the Legacy got its most important assignment yet. We drove from Christchurch to Queenstown past the impossible blue of Lake Tekapo, stayed in Arrowtown and Queenstown, and carried on to Milford Sound, coming home the long way through Dunedin. There were many trips in this car, but showing my parents a country so different from the one they raised me in, from the driver's seat of a car I had rebuilt with my own hands, was a big deal.

That trip was one role of many. Over those months the Legacy was the family car, the camper, the beach car, the fishing vehicle, the tow rig, and the roadside assist for half my friends. Whatever the plan was, the car was the main player in it.
