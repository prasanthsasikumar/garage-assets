# Subaru Legacy (2001)

## One of the Forever Cars

Some cars pass through your life. This one dug in.

On paper it is a 2001 Subaru Legacy, a sensible Japanese family wagon hiding two turbochargers under the bonnet. In practice it is the car I taught myself mechanics on, the car thieves tried twice to take from me, the car that carried my parents to Milford Sound and me through a PhD, and the one thing I refused to sell when I left New Zealand and sold everything else I owned.

I bought it for 2,500 dollars, not running, from a very cool British lady on TradeMe. What follows is the whole story: the highway tow that turned a flatmate into a brother, the restoration paid for with grocery money, the night drive that hit 180, the break-ins, the breakdown on a logging route, and the shed in Hamilton where it waits for me now.

Little did I know, when I first got the car, how much of my life it would end up holding.
