# The Restoration

This was the biggest project I had ever taken on. My entire mechanical CV at that point was one oil change. So I treated the car the way I treat any hard problem: I read everything. I printed every Legacy manual I could find in English and translated the Japanese ones, and for the next two weeks I did nothing but read and watch YouTube. If something had been written down about maintaining a second-generation Legacy, chances are I read it.

The cam belt service was long overdue, and a snapped cam belt on this engine means the engine eats itself. I decided to do the job myself. I bought every component it needed and spent every last penny I had; I genuinely did not know how I would eat the following week, but I had the car parts. Then I started slowly, removing screws and labelling them meticulously, torquing every bolt that needed it and checking each one three times to be sure the setting was right.

## Acts of Kindness

Somewhere in the middle of the job, I hit a wall. To fit the cam belt I needed to lock the camshafts in place, and that called for a special tool Subaru makes, far beyond what I could afford. Searching and posting online only confirmed it: everyone said you need the tool. So I decided to walk into a workshop, offer to pay to rent one, and ask for help.

I went to Stubaru, a Subaru specialist in Christchurch that Stu runs with his wife. Instead of turning away a broke student, he patiently explained that I didn't actually need the factory tool at all: a 100-dollar tool from a shop nearby would do the trick. He even wrote me a note to take with me. I bought it, went back to the garage, and it worked.

I grew up in India, where people are kind, but I was never used to strangers going out of their way like that. That day changed something in me. I try to be that person now whenever someone comes to me for help. Stu doesn't know it, but he made a huge impact on my life. And it kept happening; having travelled a decent amount since, I can say the kindest people live down under in Aotearoa.
